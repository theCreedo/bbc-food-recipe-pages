Lamb tagine

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Don't be put off by the long list of ingredients, tagine's are very easy and great to make-ahead if you have guests over for Christmas.

By Matt Tebbutt
From Christmas Kitchen

Ingredients

  • olive oil , for frying
  • 1kg/2lb 4oz lamb shoulder , diced
  • 2 onions , cut into thick semicircles
  • 6–8 garlic cloves, peeled and left whole
  • 2 small aubergines , diced
  • pinch saffron
  • 2 tsp coriander seeds , crushed
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds, crushed
  • few cardamom pods
  • few black peppercorns
  • pinch turmeric
  • 300ml/10fl oz dry white wine
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 1 x 400g tin chickpeas , drained
  • large handful dried apricots , cut in half
  • 500ml/18fl oz lamb or chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh mint
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh coriander

To serve

  • 250g/9oz couscous
  • pinch of saffron
  • 300ml/10fl oz hot chicken stock
  • 100g/3½oz flaked almonds , toasted
  • 100g/3½oz raisins , soaked in water
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 tbsp mixed chopped herbs, such as mint, coriander and parsley

Method

  1. For the lamb tagine, in a large ovenproof, lidded pan or casserole heat the olive oil. Season the lamb with salt and pepper. Once the oil is hot, brown the lamb on all sides for about 10 minutes. Add the onions and garlic. Cook for a few minutes to soften, then stir in the aubergines and spices and cook for a further 2 minutes.

  2. Pour in the white wine and simmer until the volume of liquid is reduced in volume by half. Stir in the tomato purée, tinned tomatoes and chickpeas and cook for a few minutes to reduce further.

  3. Add the apricots and stock. Cover the pan with a lid and cook gently for 1½–2 hours, or until the meat is very tender.

  4. Just before serving, remove the lid off and reduce the sauce a little until thickened, if necessary.

  5. Meanwhile, put the couscous in a large heatproof bowl. Mix the saffron into the hot stock then pour it over the couscous. Cover with cling film and leave for 5 minutes.

  6. Once the stock has been soaked up, mix in the flaked almonds, raisins, olive oil and chopped herbs.

  7. Serve the lamb tagine in bowls with the couscous alongside. Garnish with the chopped mint and coriander.
