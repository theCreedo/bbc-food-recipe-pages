Braised chicken wings and seared scallops with hazelnuts

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Deliciously moist chicken wings topped with seared scallops makes for a sophisticated dinner party dish.

By Bryn Williams
From 2007-16

Ingredients

For the braised chicken wings

  • 8 chicken wings , tips removed
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 50g/2oz butter
  • 1 small onion , peeled, finely diced
  • 1 medium carrot , peeled, cut into small dice
  • 1 celery stick, cut into small dice
  • 110ml/4fl oz white wine
  • 500ml/18fl oz chicken stock
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 50g/2oz hazelnuts , toasted, finely chopped

For the scallops

  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 8 medium scallops
  • 1 lemon , juice only
  • salt

Method

  1. For the braised chicken wings, season the chicken wings, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  2. Heat the butter in a frying pan until foaming and fry the chicken wings, skin-side down, for 2-3 minutes, or until golden-brown. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  3. Add the onion, carrot and celery to the pan and fry for 2-3 minutes, or until softened.

  4. Pour in the white wine, bring the mixture to the boil and cook until the volume of the liquid has reduced by half.

  5. Return the chicken wings to the pan, pour over the chicken stock and bring to the boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes. Remove the pan from the heat and set aside to cool.

  6. Once cooled, remove the chicken wings from the pan, remove the wing bones and chill the meat in the fridge.

  7. For the scallops, heat the olive oil in a frying pan and fry the scallops for 1 minute, then carefully turn the scallops over and fry for 30 seconds. Remove the scallops from the pan and season to taste, with lemon juice and salt.

  8. Add the chicken wings to the pan and fry for 2-3 minutes or until the skin is crisp.

  9. To serve, reheat the vegetables and stock and add the thyme and hazelnuts.

  10. Place 2 chicken wings and 2 scallops into each of 4 serving bowls and spoon of the stock and vegetables.
