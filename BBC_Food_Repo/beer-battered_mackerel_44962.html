Beer-battered mackerel with beetroot salad

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 2

A colourful, fresh and simple twist on classic battered fish.

By Glynn Purnell
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 150ml/5fl oz olive oil
  • 50ml/1¾fl oz balsamic vinegar
  • 1 large cooked red beetroot , cut into cubes
  • 1 medium yellow beetroot , thinly sliced
  • 1 medium candied beetroot , thinly sliced
  • ½ bunch fresh dill , roughly chopped
  • 250g/9oz crème fraîche
  • 405g/15oz plain flour , plus extra for dusting
  • 300ml/10fl oz beer
  • oil , for deep frying
  • 2 mackerel fillets, bones removed, halved lengthways
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 150g/5½oz rocket , to garnish

Method

  1. Toss the oil and vinegar with all the beetroot and season well.

  2. Mix the dill with the crème fraîche and season.

  3. Place the flour in a large bowl and whisk in the beer, a little at a time, until smooth.

  4. Preheat a deep-fat fryer to 180C (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous. Do not leave unattended).

  5. Dust the mackerel with flour, shaking off the excess, dip into the batter and fry for 3–5 minutes, or until golden-brown. Drain on kitchen paper and season well.

  6. Divide the beetroots between two plates and top with the dill crème fraîche. Place the mackerel on top and garnish with rocket.
