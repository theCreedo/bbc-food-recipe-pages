Lavender and sunflower seed brittle

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 16-20

Dietary

Vegetarian

This healthier version of peanut brittle combines lavender from the garden with toasted, lightly salted sunflower seeds.

By Jo Jo Yee
From The Big Allotment Challenge

Ingredients

  • 80g/2¾oz sunflower seeds
  • splash light soy sauce
  • 115g/4oz unsalted butter
  • 225g/8oz granulated sugar
  • 100g/3½oz light brown sugar
  • pinch salt
  • 1-2 tsp lavender flowers, finely chopped

Method

  1. Heat a frying pan over a low heat. Add the sunflower seeds and dry fry for 1-2 minutes, or until toasted. Stir in the soy sauce and cook until evaporated. Remove the seeds and set aside.

  2. Heat the butter, sugars, salt and 2 tablespoons water in a saucepan over a low heat until the butter has melted and the sugar has dissolved.

  3. Bring the mixture to the boil, then reduce the heat to moderate and continue to cook until the temperature reaches 150C. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the lavender flowers, then set aside to cool slightly.

  4. Pour the brittle mixture into silicone ice-cube trays, then sprinkle over the sunflower seeds. Set aside to harden, then remove from the moulds and serve.
