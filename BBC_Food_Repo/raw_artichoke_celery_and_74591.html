Raw artichoke, celery and broccoli salad

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Bottarga is an Italian salted, cured fish roe, usually from turbot or tuna. Here it is used to create a full-flavoured dressing for this simple yet elegant salad.

By Matt Tebbutt
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 50g/1¾oz bottarga, plus extra to garnish
  • 1 garlic clove, roughly chopped
  • 1 lemon , juice only
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • small handful cherry tomatoes , halved
  • handful basil leaves, roughly torn, plus 2 tbsp small leaves to garnish
  • 6 baby artichoke hearts, thinly sliced on a mandolin
  • 200g/7oz Tenderstem broccoli , broken into florets
  • 1 fennel bulb, thinly sliced on a mandolin
  • 1 head leafy celery , thinly sliced, leaves reserved
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • music paper bread, to serve (available from Italian delis)

Method

  1. Put the bottarga, garlic, three-quarters of the lemon juice, 2 tablespoons of the olive oil, half the tomatoes and the torn basil in a pestle and mortar and pound to a loose dressing.

  2. Toss the artichokes with the remaining lemon juice and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Blanch the broccoli in boiling water for 2 minutes, then refresh under cold running water.

  4. Arrange the broccoli, artichokes, fennel and celery on a serving plate and sprinkle over the remaining tomatoes. Drizzle over the dressing and garnish with the celery leaves and basil leaves. Finely grate over a little bottarga and serve.
