Artichoke, broad bean and pea risotto

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Dietary

Vegetarian

Make the most of vegetables with an oozy risotto, a perfect taste of Italy. If you’re lucky enough to find really tender artichokes, simply cut about ten of them in half – you won’t need to trim them.

By Rick Stein
From Rick Stein's Long Weekends

Ingredients

  • 1 lemon, juice only or 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 3 globe artichokes
  • 1.2 litres/2 pints vegetable stock
  • 40g/1½oz butter
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion , chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped or grated
  • 300g/10½oz Arborio or carnaroli rice
  • 125ml/4fl oz dry white wine
  • 200g/7oz fresh broad beans , double podded if not young (600–700g/1lb 5oz–1lb 9oz weight in pods)
  • 200g/7oz fresh peas , podded (about 600g/1lb 5oz weight in pods)
  • ½ small unwaxed lemon , juice and grated zest only
  • ½–1 tsp salt
  • 8 turns black pepper mill
  • 70g/2½oz freshly grated pecorino (or other vegetarian hard cheese)

Method

  1. First prepare the globe artichokes. Put the lemon juice or vinegar in a bowl of cold water. Remove the stem of the artichoke to within 1cm/½in of the base. Peel off the outer leaves until you reach the pale coloured centre. Cut the body of the artichoke in half horizontally. Remove the hairy choke with a teaspoon. Pare off any remaining outer leaves with a sharp knife. Cut into six wedges and put them into the lemon water to stop them going brown.

  2. Heat the stock in a saucepan.

  3. Melt the half the butter with the olive oil in a wide pan over medium-low heat and sweat the onion and garlic for 5–10 minutes until soft.

  4. Add the rice and stir well to coat the grains. Add the white wine and stir until it has been absorbed by the rice. Start adding the vegetable stock a ladleful at a time, stirring until it is absorbed, which will take about 20 minutes. The rice should be cooked through but retain bite (you may not need all of the stock).

  5. After about 12 minutes, bring a pot of water to the boil, add the artichoke and cook for 5–7 minutes until tender. Remove and set aside. Add the broad beans and peas and boil for about 3 minutes, then drain. Add all the vegetables to the risotto and heat through.

  6. Remove from the heat and stir through the lemon zest, juice and remaining butter. Season with salt, pepper and half the pecorino. Serve immediately with the remaining pecorino.
