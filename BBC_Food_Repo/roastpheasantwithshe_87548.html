Roast pheasant with sherry glazed parsnips and chestnuts

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 2

By Lawrence Keogh
From 2007-16

Ingredients

For the pheasant

  • pheasant , preferably hen as they are fattier
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 50g/2oz butter

For the parsnips and chestnuts

  • 800g/1¾lb parsnips , peeled, cut into 7cm/3in batons
  • 250ml/9fl oz dry sherry , such as fino or manzanilla
  • 75g/3oz soft brown sugar
  • 100g/3½oz butter
  • 10 chestnuts , roasted, peeled, roughly chopped
  • small bunch flatleaf parsley , chopped
  • ½ bunch watercress , to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 210C/410F/Gas 6.

  2. For the pheasant, remove the legs and wishbone of the pheasant. Season thoroughly inside and out with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Reserve the legs for use in another dish.

  3. Heat a frying pan until hot, add the butter, place the pheasant in the pan and sear on each side until golden.

  4. Place in the oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until cooked to your liking. Remove from the oven and rest.

  5. For the parsnips and chestnuts, place the parsnips, sherry, sugar and butter into an earthenware dish or roasting tin and mix together to combine.

  6. Place in the oven for 15-20 minutes until golden and tender.

  7. Remove and place on the hob, bring to a simmer then reduce the liquid down to a thick glaze. Add the chestnuts and parsley and mix together. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  8. To serve, carve the breast from the pheasant. Place the parsnips and chestnuts onto the centre of each serving plate. Top with the pheasant and a handful of watercress.
