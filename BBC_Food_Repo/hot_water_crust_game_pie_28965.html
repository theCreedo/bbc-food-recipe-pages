Hot water crust game pie

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Serves 6-8

Try this game terrine filling wrapped in delicious hot water crust pastry for winter buffets or a Boxing Day spread.

Equipment and prepration: you will need a 23x10cm/9x4in terrine mould.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the hot water crust pastry

  • 200g/7oz plain flour
  • 40g/1½oz strong white flour
  • 50g/1¾oz butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 60g/2¼oz lard
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten, for egg wash

For the game terrine

  • 1kg/2lb 4oz game meat (such as breast of pheasant, pigeon, duck or lean venison), diced
  • 500g/1lb 2oz sausage meat
  • 200g/7oz chicken livers , finely chopped
  • 2 handfuls white breadcrumbs
  • 1 free-range egg , beaten
  • 3 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • few sprigs fresh thyme , leaves picked
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • splash brandy
  • splash red wine

For the shallot chutney

  • 50g/1¾oz butter
  • 8 banana shallots , sliced into rings
  • 75g/2½oz dark brown sugar
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme , leaves picked
  • 200ml/7fl oz balsamic vinegar

Method

  1. For the hot water crust pastry, combine the flours in a large mixing bowl, then rub the butter in with your fingertips.

  2. Mix 130ml/4¼fl oz boiling water with the salt and lard in a small saucepan. Gently heat until the lard melts, stirring all the time to combine.

  3. Pour the lard and water mixture on top of the flour mixture, stirring with a spoon until everything comes together to form a soft dough.

  4. Tip the dough onto a lightly floured work surface and bring together into a ball. Leave to rest for about 10 minutes until cool enough to handle.

  5. Take one-third of the pastry, wrap in cling film and transfer to the fridge to chill. Line a terrine mould with baking paper. Roll out the larger piece of pastry to fit the terrine mould. Lay the pastry into the baking paper and use your hands to pull the pastry up the sides so it lines the mould up to the top. Place in the fridge for 20 minutes.

  6. For the game terrine, preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4. Put all the ingredients in a bowl and mix to combine.

  7. Roll out the reserved third of pastry to form the pie lid and set aside.

  8. Fill the chilled terrine mould with the game filling and place the pastry top on the pie. Crimp round the sides, to seal thoroughly. Brush the lid of the pie with the egg wash.

  9. Bake for 1-1¼ hours.

  10. For the shallot chutney, place the butter in a large frying pan and heat until melted. Add the shallots, cover and cook on a low heat until the shallots have softened and caramelised.

  11. Add the sugar, thyme and vinegar and cook for a further 10 minutes.

  12. To serve, cut a few slices of the game terrine pie and serve with the melba toast and shallot chutney.
