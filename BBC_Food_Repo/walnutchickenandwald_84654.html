Walnut chicken and Waldorf salad

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Homemade mayonnaise transforms this chicken and Waldorf salad, but if you're in a hurry you can use good quality shopbought.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the chicken

  • 110g/4oz walnuts , roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts , sliced thickly
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 tbsp olive oil

For the salad

  • 1 head celery
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 110g/4oz walnut halves
  • 2 green apples , cut into wedges
  • 2 red apples , cut into wedges
  • 1 lemon , zest and juice only

For the mayonnaise

  • 2 free-range egg yolks
  • 1 tsp English mustard
  • 1-2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 150ml/5fl oz sunflower oil
  • 150ml/5fl oz olive oil
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Place the walnuts into a food processor and blend to fine crumbs.

  2. Brush the Dijon mustard onto the chicken fillet slices and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  3. Place the ground walnuts into a large bowl. Add the chicken slices and roll in the walnuts to coat completely.

  4. Heat a frying pan until hot then add the olive oil. Place the chicken into the frying pan and fry for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden and completely cooked through. Remove from the pan and drain onto kitchen paper.

  5. For the salad, chop half of the celery into small chunks, and slice the remainder into 5cm/2in long strips.

  6. Place the olive oil and thyme leaves into a bowl, then add the celery strips. Toss the celery to coat in the thyme oil and season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  7. Heat a griddle pan until smoking. Place the celery strips onto the griddle and char for 1-2 minutes, until marked by the griddle and heated through.

  8. Place the celery chunks and remaining salad ingredients into a bowl and mix well.

  9. For the mayonnaise, place the egg yolks, mustard and white wine vinegar into a food processor and blend.

  10. With the processor still running, slowly pour in the sunflower oil and olive oil, drop by drop at first, then faster as the mixture thickens, alternating between the two oils as you go. Continue blending until all the oil is incorporated and a thick mayonnaise is formed. Transfer the mayonnaise to a clean bowl and season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  11. To serve, add the mayonnaise to the bowl of salad and mix well. Place the salad mixture into the centre of each of four plates, top each with pieces of the walnut chicken and pieces of griddled celery.
