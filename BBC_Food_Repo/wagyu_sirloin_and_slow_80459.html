Wagyu sirloin and slow-cooked brisket, sprouting broccoli, wagyu tartare on puffed heel tendon

Preparation time

overnight

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

This recipe includes a number of ingredients that are difficult to source, but you can take inspiration from some of the simpler elements when cooking at home.

Equipment and preparation: for this recipe you will need a dehydrator and a deep-fat fryer.

By Paul Foster
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the tartare

  • 4 egg yolks
  • sea salt , for curing
  • 2 braised and dried beef heel tendons
  • 1litre/1¾ pints flavourless vegetable oil , for deep frying
  • 100g/3½oz Wagyu sirloin
  • 1 tsp finely chopped banana shallot
  • 10 leaves flatleaf parsley , thinly shredded

For the brisket

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil , for frying
  • 200g/7oz onions , roughly chopped
  • 1kg/2lb 4oz Wagyu brisket
  • 250ml/9fl oz light bitter ale
  • 500ml/18fl oz brown chicken stock
  • 50g/1¾oz clear honey
  • cabernet sauvignon vinegar , to taste

For the dressing

  • 6 smoked garlic cloves
  • 100ml/3½fl oz white wine vinegar
  • 50g/1¾oz caster sugar
  • 50ml/2fl oz parsley oil

For the Wagyu sirloin

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil , for frying
  • 200g/7oz Wagyu sirloin
  • 50g/1¾oz butter

For the sprouting broccoli

  • 200g/7oz sprouting broccoli , trimmed, florets, leaves and stalks separated
  • 50g/1¾oz butter

Method

  1. For the tartare, first cure the egg yolks. Put a layer of salt in the bottom of a small container, lay the egg yolks on top and cover with more salt. Cover and put in the fridge for 12 hours.

  2. Wash the egg yolks gently in cold water and place in a dehydrator set as 40C for 24 hours.

  3. Boil the beef heel tendons for 4 hours until very soft. Place on a tray and leave to cool and firm up. Slice thinly and dehydrate until hard.

  4. Fill a deep-fat fryer with oil and heat to 180C. (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous. Do not leave unattended.) Add the beef heel tendons and fry until light and fluffy. Remove the beef using a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper. Season with sea salt and set aside.

  5. Chop the Wagyu sirloin into 5mm/¼in squares. Mix with the shallot and parsley and season with sea salt. Set aside until needed.

  6. For the brisket, preheat the oven to 140C/120C Fan/Gas 1. Heat the oil in a large casserole. Add the onions and brisket and fry for a few minutes, until the meat is browned all over and the onions are soft. Pour in the ale, stock and honey. Cover with a lid and bake for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender. Carefully remove the brisket from the casserole and keep warm.

  7. Pass the cooking liquid through a fine sieve into a saucepan. Cook over a medium heat until reduced to a glaze. Season, to taste, with sea salt and cabernet sauvignon vinegar.

  8. For the dressing, fry the smoked garlic in a hot pan in a splash of oil until golden-brown, then reduce the heat and cook until soft. Add the vinegar and sugar and bring to the boil. Remove from the heat.

  9. Transfer the garlic to a blender, add most of the cooking liquid and blend until smooth. Add a little more cooking liquid if needed to achieve a smooth dressing (it should be fairly thick as you will add the parsley oil at the last minute). Set aside until needed.

  10. For the Wagyu sirloin, heat the oil in heavy-based frying pan. When hot, add the Wagyu sirloin and fry for 15-20 seconds. Turn and fry the other side for 15-20 seconds. Repeat this process for 2-3 minutes, adding the butter for the final minute. (This will keep the heat in the pan and build up a nice crust while keeping the centre moist and pink.) Set aside on a wire rack to rest for 5 minutes.

  11. For the sprouting broccoli, blanch the stalks for 2 minutes, the florets for 1 minute and the leaves for 30 seconds. Dress with butter and season with sea salt.

  12. To serve, glaze the brisket in the reduced cooking liquor. Carve the Wagyu steak and season with salt.

  13. Divide the brisket, steak and broccoli between four serving plates. Mix the parsley oil with the smoked garlic dressing and drizzle over the steak.

  14. Spoon the tartare onto the puffed beef heel tendon, grate over the cured egg yolks and serve on the side.
