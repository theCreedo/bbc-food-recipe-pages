Cauliflower cheese

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4-6

A perfect side dish for roast chicken – either as part of a traditional roast or with cold leftover chicken.

By The Hairy Bikers
From Hairy Bikers - Chicken & Egg

Ingredients

  • 1 large cauliflower , cut into florets

For the sauce

  • 500ml/18fl oz milk
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 blade mace
  • few peppercorns
  • 30g/1oz butter
  • 30g/1oz plain flour
  • ½ tsp mustard powder
  • pinch cayenne pepper
  • 100g/3½oz cheddar or gruyère
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/160C Fan/Gas 3.

  2. Bring a large saucepan of salted water to the boil. Add the cauliflower and cook for 4 minutes, or until tender. Drain and put in a large ovenproof dish.

  3. Pour the milk into a saucepan and add the bay leaves, mace and peppercorns. Bring almost to the boil, then remove from the heat and set aside.

  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter. Stir in the flour, mustard powder and cayenne pepper. Beat until smooth, then cook for a further 1–2 minutes, stirring all the time. Strain the milk into a jug and gradually add it to the pan, beating in each addition until smooth before adding more milk. As the mixture loosens you can add the milk in larger quantities, stirring constantly until it is all incorporated. Add 75g/2½oz cheese and stir until melted. Season with salt and pepper.

  5. Pour the sauce over the cauliflower and sprinkle with the reserved cheese. Bake for around 30 minutes, or until the top is golden-brown and bubbling.
