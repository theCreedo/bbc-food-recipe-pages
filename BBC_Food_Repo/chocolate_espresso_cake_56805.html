Chocolate espresso cake

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 8

Dietary

Vegetarian

Cook this indulgent chocolate and almond cake so that it is just firm, but still a little squidgy on the inside. Delicious with a dollop of whipped cream and a sweet espresso sauce.

Equipment and preparation: You will need a 20cm/8in non-stick spring-form tin.

By Lesley Waters
From Chefs: Put Your Menu Where Your Mouth Is

Ingredients

  • 185g/6½oz butter , softened
  • 185g/6½oz dark chocolate
  • 340g/11¾oz caster sugar
  • 3 free-range eggs
  • 140g/5oz ground almonds
  • 200g/7oz self-raising flour
  • 250ml/9fl oz hot strong espresso, sweetened with 2 tbsp caster sugar
  • whipped cream , to serve

Method

  1. Preheat a fan assisted oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. Lightly oil a 20cm/8in non-stick spring-form tin and line the base with baking paper.

  3. Gently heat the butter and chocolate over a low heat and stir until melted.

  4. Beat together the sugar and eggs until pale and thick, using an electric beater.

  5. Quickly fold the chocolate, almonds and flour into the egg mixture until smooth.

  6. Spoon the mixture into the prepared cake tin and bake in the oven for 50 minutes to one hour, or until just firm.

  7. When cooked, remove the cake from the oven and leave to cool for 20 minutes before removing from the tin.

  8. Meanwhile, place the sweetened espresso coffee into a saucepan, bring to the boil and simmer for a minute until syrupy.

  9. Remove the baking paper from the cake and place it on a serving plate.

  10. Serve the warm cake with a dollop of whipped cream and a trickle of the hot espresso sauce.
