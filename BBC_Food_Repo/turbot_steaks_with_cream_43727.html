Turbot steaks with cream, baked carrots, orange and wild sorrel

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Turbot in a creamy sauce served with buttered spinach and orange scented carrots.

By Paul Rankin
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the carrot and orange vinaigrette

  • 4 medium carrots
  • 2 lemons , juice of both and zest of one
  • 1 tsp marmalade
  • 1 tsp sriracha chilli sauce
  • 8 tbsp cold pressed rapeseed oil

For the turbot steaks with cream, baked carrots, orange and wild sorrel

  • 50g/1¾oz unsalted butter
  • 2 shallots , finely chopped
  • 4 mushrooms , finely sliced
  • 4 turbot steaks (approximately 150g/5½oz each)
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 125ml/4fl oz white wine
  • 200ml/7oz double cream
  • 4 tbsp chopped sorrel

To serve

  • 500g/1lb 2oz large-leaf spinach
  • 50g/1¾oz unsalted butter
  • handful wild sorrel leaves

Method

  1. For the carrots and orange vinaigrette, preheat the oven to 150C/300F/Gas 2.

  2. Lay a large piece of aluminium foil on a baking sheet and place the carrots on top, fold the foil over to make a parcel and place in the oven for 1½ hours, or until the carrots are tender.

  3. Once cooked, set aside to cool. Then cut the carrots lengthways and place in a shallow container.

  4. Place the remaining vinaigrette ingredients in a small bowl and whisk to combine. Pour over the carrots and set aside to marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  5. For the turbot steaks with cream, baked carrots, orange and wild sorrel, add a little butter to an ovenproof frying pan set over a medium heat. Add the shallots and mushrooms and place the turbot steaks on top. Pour in the wine and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Cover loosely with a buttered piece of baking parchment and place in the oven for 8-10 minutes. Turn the fish over half way through cooking.

  6. Once cooked, remove the fish from the pan and keep warm until ready to serve. Place the frying pan over a medium heat and add the cream and sorrel. Simmer until the liquid has reduced by half. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Pass the sauce through a sieve and discard the vegetables.

  7. To serve, fill a large pan with boiling water and plunge the spinach into the water then drain.

  8. Place a frying pan over a medium heat and add the butter, once hot add the spinach and cook for two minutes.

  9. Put the wilted spinach in the centre of serving plates and place the turbot on top. Add the carrots to the side. Spoon the sauce over the fish and finish with a drizzle of orange dressing and a few wild sorrel leaves.
