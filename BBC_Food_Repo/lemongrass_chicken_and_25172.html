Lemongrass chicken and aubergine and warm spring onion vinaigrette

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 2

Cooking aubergine over a open flame gives it a delicious smokey flavour which complements the freshness of spring onions and lemongrass.

By Bobby Chinn
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the chicken thighs

  • 4 chicken thighs , skinless and boneless
  • 4 tbsp chopped lemongrass (very finely chop the bottom part of the lemongrass )
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp fish sauce

For the fish caramel sauce

  • 35g/1¼oz dark palm sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce

For the smoky aubergine

  • 1 Japanese long aubergine
  • 10g/⅓oz finely chopped shallot
  • 1 tbsp chopped spring onion (green part only)
  • 1 shallot , sliced into rings
  • sea salt

For the spring onion vinaigrette

  • 75g/2½oz pickling liqueur (equal quantities of caster sugar and water, flavoured with a smashed garlic clove)
  • 75g/2½oz Vietnamese chilli paste

To serve

  • 2 lime leaves , thinly sliced
  • jasmine rice , to serve

Method

  1. For the chicken thighs, put the chicken in a bowl and add half the lemongrass and oil, and all the fish sauce. Leave to marinade for 20 minutes, or longer if convenient.

  2. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  3. For the fish caramel sauce, heat a flameproof casserole. Add a tablespoon of water and the palm sugar. Cook over a low heat until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture has a syrup-like consistency.

  4. Set aside to cool for two minutes, then add the fish sauce and marinated chicken. Return to the heat and cook for 5-8 minutes. Transfer to the oven to finish.

  5. For the smokey aubergine, heat a deep-fat fryer to 180C/350F. (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous. Do not leave unattended.)

  6. Pierce the aubergine all over with a fork and place over an open flame until it is blistered and burnt all over and tender inside. Set aside to cool a little.

  7. Meanwhile, for the spring onion vinaigrette place all the ingredients in a pan with 460ml/16fl oz water. Heat gently.

  8. For the smoky aubergine, add the finely chopped shallot and spring onions to the spring onion vinaigrette.

  9. Place the shallot rings in the deep fat fryer and deep fry until crisp. Drain and place onto a plate lined with kitchen roll to drain.

  10. To finish, top and tail the aubergine then slice it down the middle lengthways. Scoop out the tender flesh from the burnt skin and divide between serving plates. Use scissors to cut it into bite-sized pieces.

  11. Take the spring onions out of the vinaigrette and drain them, reserving the vinaigrette for dressing.

  12. Garnish with the blanched spring onions, crisp shallots and lime leaves. Dress with the vinaigrette. Serve the chicken in the casserole with jasmine rice.
