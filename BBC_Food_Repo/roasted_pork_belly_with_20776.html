Roasted pork belly with brioche and leek bake

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Indulge in crispy crackling and succulent pork alongside a decadent brioche and leek bake.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the roasted pork belly

  • 1.5kg/3lb 4oz pork belly
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 onion , chopped
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 200ml/7fl oz dry white wine
  • 150ml/5fl oz chicken stock

For the brioche and leek bake

  • 75g/3oz butter
  • 500g/1lb 2oz leeks , sliced
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme , leaves picked
  • 150ml/5fl oz white wine
  • 200ml/7fl oz double cream
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 75g/3oz brioche loaf, cut into 1cm cubes
  • 50g/2oz Gruyère , finely grated

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 230C/450F/Gas 8.

  2. Score the fat of the pork belly, roll up tightly and secure with several pieces of string. Rub the skin with the olive oil and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  3. Place the onion and thyme in a flameproof baking tray and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Place the pork on top and cook in the oven for 30 minutes, or until the skin is crispy and golden-brown.

  4. Reduce the oven temperature to 170C/325F/Gas 3. Add 200ml/7fl oz water and the white wine and cook for a further 2 hours.

  5. Remove the tray from the oven, remove the pork and set aside to rest for 15 minutes. Turn the oven up to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  6. Place the baking tray onto the heat, add the stock and bring to the boil, using a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper and strain the gravy into a jug.

  7. Meanwhile for the brioche and leek bake, heat 50g/2oz of the butter in a frying pan and fry the leeks and thyme for 2-3 minutes, or until softened.

  8. Add the wine and cook until the volume of the liquid has reduced by two-thirds, add the cream and reduce by half again. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper and pour into an ovenproof dish.

  9. Heat a separate frying pan until hot. Add the remaining butter and the brioche, and cook until golden-brown on all sides. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper and scatter over the top of the leeks.

  10. Sprinkle the cheese on top of the dish and cook in the oven for 5-10 minutes, or until golden-brown and bubbling.

  11. To serve, carve the pork into thick slices. Spoon the brioche and leek bake onto serving plates and place the pork belly alongside. Pour over the gravy.
