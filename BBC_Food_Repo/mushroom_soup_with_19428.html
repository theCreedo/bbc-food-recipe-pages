Mushroom soup with garlic bread

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Dietary

Vegetarian

You can use any type of mushrooms you have for this slinky smooth soup. The herby topping and garlic bread brings a delightful crunch. Recipe inspired by Saturday Kitchen viewer Rebecca Collins.

By Matt Tebbutt
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 shallots , roughly chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves , finely chopped
  • 400g/14oz various different mushrooms , roughly chopped
  • 4 fresh thyme sprigs
  • 150ml/5fl oz double cream

For the topping

  • 1 red onion , thinly sliced
  • 150g/5½oz various different mushrooms , roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh tarragon

For the garlic bread

  • ¼ sourdough loaf, torn into pie ces
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 100g/3½oz Parmesan cheese (or other vegetarian hard cheese), grated, to serve
  • ½ truffle , freshly shaved (optional), to serve

Method

  1. To make the soup, heat the oil in a sauté pan over a medium heat. Add the shallots, garlic, mushrooms and thyme and cook 2–3 minutes. Add 500ml/18fl oz water,

  2. bring to the boil and cook for a further 3 minutes. Add the cream and blitz in a blender until smooth.

  3. To make the topping, heat the oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Add the onion and mushrooms and cook for 2 minutes. Add the herbs and mix to combine.

  4. To make the bread, heat the oil in a frying pan over a medium heat. Add the bread and garlic and cook until crispy. Remove from the pan and allow to drain on kitchen paper.

  5. To serve, pour the soup into serving bowls and top with the garnish and garlic bread. Garnish with the parmesan and truffle shavings.
