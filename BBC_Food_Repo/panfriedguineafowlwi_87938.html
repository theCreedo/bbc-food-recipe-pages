Pan-fried guinea fowl with bubble and squeak, lardons chipolatas and bread sauce

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

4

By Mark Sargeant
From 2007-16

Ingredients

For the guinea fowl

  • 1.5 litres/2 pints 13fl oz chicken stock
  • 2 guinea fowl crowns (the main body of the bird with legs and wings removed)
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 25g/1oz butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For the bubble and squeak

  • 750g/1lb 10oz floury potatoes (such as King Edwards or Maris Piper), peeled and cut into chunks
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 300g/11oz Savoy cabbage , inside leaves only, finely sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • nutmeg , freshly grated

For the chipolatas

  • 25g/1oz butter
  • 300g/11oz cocktail sausages (chipolatas)
  • 12 baby onions , peeled
  • 200g/7oz streaky bacon, cut into lardons
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 75ml/3fl oz sherry vinegar

For the bread sauce

  • 1 large onion
  • 6 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 450ml/1 pint whole milk
  • 200g/7oz fresh brioche, blended into breadcrumbs
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. For the guinea fowl, place the chicken stock into a saucepan and add the guinea fowl, thyme and garlic. Bring to the boil then reduce the heat to simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the guinea fowl are completely cooked through.

  2. Remove the guinea fowl from the cooking liquid and pat dry with kitchen paper. Remove the breasts from the carcass and set aside. (You can use the carcasses for another purpose, such as making stock.)

  3. Heat a frying pan until hot, then add the butter and olive oil. Add the guinea fowl and pan-fry on each side until golden-brown on both sides. Remove and set aside in a warm place.

  4. For the bubble and squeak, place the potatoes into a saucepan and cover with water. Add a little salt and bring to the boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15 minutes, or until cooked through.

  5. Drain and return the cooked potatoes to the pan over a low heat to drive off any excess moisture. Mash the potatoes lightly and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  6. Meanwhile, place the Savoy cabbage into a heatproof bowl and cover with boiling water. Leave for 1-2 minutes, then drain and pat dry with kitchen paper.

  7. Heat a frying pan until hot, then add the olive oil, potatoes and cabbage. Stir fry for 4-5 minutes, until crisp and golden all over. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper and freshly grated nutmeg.

  8. For the chipolatas, heat a clean frying pan until hot. Then add the butter, cocktail sausages and onions and cook for 1-2 minutes, until just starting to turn golden-brown.

  9. Add the bacon lardons and thyme and cook for 4-5 minutes, until the lardons are golden-brown and the chipolatas are completely cooked through.

  10. Add the sherry vinegar and stir to scrape up any cooking residue from the pan (this is called deglazing). Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  11. For the bread sauce, stud the onion with the cloves then place into a pan. Add the bay leaf and milk and place over a high heat. Bring to the boil, then remove from the heat and allow to cool.

  12. Remove the onion and bay leaf from the milk and add the brioche crumbs. Place back onto a very low heat for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally until the brioche crumbs have absorbed all of the liquid.

  13. Stir in the butter and season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  14. To serve, spoon some bubble and squeak onto each plate and top each with a guinea fowl breast. Spoon the chipolata mixture alongside, add a spoonful of bread sauce and serve.
