Rump of lamb, sprouting broccoli and asparagus, new potatoes and broccoli purée

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

A delightful springtime dinner for two using some of the very best of English produce.

Ingredients

  • 100ml/3½fl oz rapeseed oil , plus extra for drizzling
  • 100g/3½oz new potatoes
  • 1 x 175g/6oz rump of lamb
  • 1 head broccoli , florets broken off, stalk removed
  • 100g/3½oz purple sprouting broccoli
  • 100g/3½oz white sprouting broccoli
  • 100g/3½oz asparagus , woody ends removed
  • 100g/3½oz unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 190C/170C Fan/Gas 5. Boil the potatoes in lightly salted water until cooked through. Drain and leave to cool, then slice them thinly.

  2. Heat half the oil in a large frying pan and sauté the potatoes for 5–10 minutes, or until golden-brown on both sides.

  3. Meanwhile, heat the remaining oil in a large, ovenproof frying pan over a high heat. Sear the lamb for 1 minute on each side, until the fat has rendered. Transfer to the oven for 5 minutes, then leave to rest for 3 minutes.

  4. Meanwhile, boil the broccoli florets in salted water for 4 minutes. Drain and blend with a hand-held blender until smooth. Check the seasoning.

  5. Boil the asparagus, purple and white sprouting broccoli in salted water for 2–3 minutes. Drain.

  6. Heat the butter in a large frying pan. When foaming, add the garlic and rosemary and cook for 1 minute. Pour this over the lamb and carve into thick slices.

  7. To serve, place the broccoli purée in the centre of a serving dish. Scatter over the new potatoes and arrange the lamb slices around them. Nestle in the broccoli and asparagus spears and drizzle with rapeseed oil.
