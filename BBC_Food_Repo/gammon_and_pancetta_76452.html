Gammon and pancetta fidget pie

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

A hearty pie that hails from Shropshire - serve hot or cold as part of a picnic.

By Theo Randall
From Country Show Cook Off

Ingredients

For the pastry

  • 500g/1lb 2oz plain flour , plus extra for dusting
  • 250g/9oz unsalted butter , chopped
  • 2 free-range egg yolks
  • 5 tbsp iced water
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten with a little milk

For the filling

  • 3 Maris Piper potatoes , peeled and cut into 1cm/½in slices
  • 1 onion , finely sliced
  • 150g/5½oz pancetta
  • 300g/10½oz gammon
  • 4 sage leaves, finely sliced
  • oil , to taste
  • 2 bramley apples , peeled cored and cut into 1 cm slices
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp muscovado sugar
  • 250ml/9fl oz cider
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 250ml/9fl oz double cream

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4.

  2. For the pastry, place all the ingredients – except the iced water - into a food processor. Pulse until you have mixture which resembles breadcrumbs. Add the water and pulse to combine. Wrap the dough in clingfilm and place in the fridge to chill until needed.

  3. For the filling, blanch the potato slices in boiling salted water for a few minutes.

  4. Meanwhile, gently fry the onion until soft and translucent. Then add the pancetta and fry until crisp.

  5. In a clean pan, fry the gammon and sage for 1-2 minutes. Remove from the pan and leave to until the gammon is cool enough to handle, then roughly chop or tear it into small pieces.

  6. In a shallow ovenproof dish, drizzle a little oil, add a layer of potatoes and top with some gammon. Next add a layer of the onion and pancetta mix and a layer of apples to the dish. Now add a layer of fried onion and another layer of gammon. Finally, add another layer of the onion and pancetta mix. Season with freshly ground black pepper and add a sprinkle of sugar.

  7. Mix the cider, cornflour and double cream together. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper, then pour over the layers of filling.

  8. Take chilled pastry from the fridge and, on a lightly floured work surface, roll out pastry to the thickness of a pound coin. Place the pastry on top of the filling in the ovenproof dish, crimp the edges and make a hole in the centre, so that moisture can escape.

  9. Brush the pastry with the beaten egg and bake for an hour, or until the pastry is crisp and a rich golden-brown.
