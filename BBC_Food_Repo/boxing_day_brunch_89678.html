Boxing Day brunch ‘kedgeree’

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Nathan Outlaw turns leftover Christmas meats into a delicious dish that will fill up the family after a Boxing Day walk.

By Nathan Outlaw
From Christmas Kitchen

Ingredients

  • drizzle light rapeseed oil
  • 50g/1¾oz unsalted butter
  • 2 shallots , finely chopped
  • 1 leek , white part only, thinly sliced
  • 1 celery stalk, thinly sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped
  • 300g/10½oz long-grain or basmati rice , washed and drained
  • 700ml/1 pint 4½fl oz chicken or turkey stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • pinch saffron strands
  • ½ tsp medium curry powder
  • 200g/7oz cooked leftover turkey , leg meat only, cut into pieces
  • 200g/7oz cooked leftover ham , cut into pieces
  • 200g/7oz cooked leftover sausages or sausage meat, cut into pieces
  • 3 free-range eggs
  • 2 tsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tsp chopped fresh sage
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • lemon wedges, to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6.

  2. Heat the oil and butter in an ovenproof, lidded saucepan over a medium heat. When the butter is foaming, add the shallots, leek, celery and garlic and fry for 2-3 minutes, stirring well, until softened but not coloured.

  3. Add the rice and continue to cook, stirring well, for a 1 minute.

  4. Pour in the stock, then add the bay leaves, saffron and curry powder and heat until simmering.

  5. Add the leftover cooked meats, return the mixture to a simmer, then cover the pan with the lid.

  6. Transfer the pan to the oven and cook for 12-15 minutes, until the rice is tender.

  7. Meanwhile, carefully lower the eggs into a pan of simmering water and cook for 6 minutes. Drain well and run the eggs under a cold running tap. Peel the eggs and cut them into quarters lengthways.

  8. When the kedgeree is cooked, stir through the parsley and sage, then fold in the egg quarters. Season with salt and pepper.

  9. To serve, take the pan to the table and spoon it into bowls. Garnish with a lemon wedge and serve with bread and butter, if desired.
