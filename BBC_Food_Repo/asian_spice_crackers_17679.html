Asian spice crackers

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 30-40

Crisp salty-sweet crackers spiced with garlic, cumin and coriander. Perfect for snacking or serving with dips.

By John Whaite
From The Great British Bake Off

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp black pepper corns
  • ½ beef stock cube
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 90g/3¼oz butter
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • ½ garlic clove
  • 300g/10½oz plain flour , plus extra for dusting

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 210C/425F/Gas 7. Line a baking tray with non-stick parchment.

  2. Grind the spices in a pestle and mortar to a coarse powder, then transfer to a food processor and blend with the stock cube, salt and sugar.

  3. Melt the butter together with 90ml/3¼fl oz water and the sesame oil. Pour the mixture into a bowl and stir in the spices and garlic. Finally add the flour and mix well to combine.

  4. Roll the dough out on a lightly floured surface to a ½cm/¼in thickness, prick evenly all over with a fork, and cut out in 5cm/2in circles. Transfer the crackers to the lined baking tray.

  5. Bake for 9-12 minutes until golden-brown and crisp.
