Black Forest rolls

Preparation time

1-2 hours

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Makes about 18 bread rolls

Dietary

Vegetarian

Dark chocolate and cherries bring a taste of the Black Forest to these sweet bread rolls.

By Luis Troyano
From The Great British Bake Off

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 150ml/5fl oz whole milk
  • ¼ tsp fine sea salt
  • 75g/2½oz caster sugar
  • 1 medium free-range egg
  • 1 orange , zest only
  • 1 unwaxed lemon , zest only
  • 600g/1lb 5oz strong white flour , plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tsp fast-action yeast
  • 100g/3½oz unsalted butter , softened
  • 1 tsp rapeseed oil , for greasing

For the filling

  • 200g/7oz dried morello cherries
  • 75g/2½oz dark Belgian chocolate chips, chilled in the fridge
  • 20 white sugar cubes, halved
  • 4 tbsp cherry brandy

For the decoration

  • 1 large free-range egg, beaten, for egg wash
  • 20 coloured glacé cherries , cut in half
  • 100g/3½fl oz nibbed sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon , for dusting
  • 50g/1¾oz good-quality plain chocolate (70% cocoa solids)

Method

  1. For the dough, put the milk in a heatproof jug and heat it in a microwave until boiling. Add 150ml/5½fl oz cold water to cool it down.

  2. In the bowl of a freestanding mixer with a dough hook attachment, add the salt, sugar, egg, orange zest, lemon zest, flour and yeast. (Make sure the yeast and salt do not touch.)

  3. Add approximately half the milk mixture and, using the dough hook attachment, mix slowly to bring the mixture together. Keep adding the milk mixture until all the flour is incorporated (you may not need all the milk). Increase the mixer speed to medium and mix for three minutes.

  4. Slowly add the butter until incorporated and mix for a further six minutes. The dough should be smooth and elastic.

  5. Grease a large bowl with rapeseed oil and add the dough. Cover with a shower cap or greased cling film. Prove the dough in a warm place (ideally 27C/80F) for 50 minutes, or until the dough has doubled in size.

  6. When the dough has proved, tip it out onto a lightly floured work surface. Knock back the dough and fold over on itself a couple of times.

  7. For the filling, gently knead in the cherries and chocolate chips until evenly distributed. Divide the dough into 80g/2¾oz portions and roll into balls.

  8. Flatten the dough balls slightly and place half a sugar cube in the centre of each ball. Soak each sugar cube with some cherry brandy, using a pipette if available. Pull the dough over the sugar cubes, to seal the cube in the centre. Roll into neat balls.

  9. Line two large baking trays with non-stick baking parchment. Place the dough balls on the baking tray leaving a 1cm/½in gap between the balls to allow for expansion. Spray with a fine mist of water (if you don’t have a spray bottle, wet your hands and flick droplets of water onto the buns). Prove for another 25 minutes in a warm place (ideally 27C/80F).

  10. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4 and place a rack on the third shelf (just below the centre).

  11. For the decoration, after proving, gently brush the buns all over with egg wash. Place a glace cherry on top of each bun and decorate with nibbed sugar. Using a small sieve, dust the buns with ground cinnamon.

  12. Bake for around 25–30 minutes, or until well risen and golden-brown. Remove from the oven and place on a wire rack to cool.

  13. Meanwhile, melt the dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl suspended over a pan of gently simmering water (do not allow the bowl to touch the water). Spoon the chocolate into a piping bag fitted with a fine writing nozzle.

  14. When cooled, drizzle the melted chocolate over the buns to decorate. Serve once the chocolate is set.
