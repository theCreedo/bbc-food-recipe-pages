Pan-fried gurnard with broad bean, spinach and mint stew

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

This light stew uses the highly underrated fish gurnard, and is full of summer vegetables.

By Cass Titcombe
From 2007-16

Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 200g/7oz new potatoes , lightly scraped then quartered lengthways
  • ½ onion , peeled, sliced finely
  • 1 garlic clove, peeled, finely chopped
  • 400ml/14floz chicken stock
  • salt , to taste
  • 200g/7oz frozen shelled and podded broad beans , thawed
  • 250g/9oz spinach , chopped
  • 10g/½ oz fresh mint leaves, sliced finely
  • freshly ground black pepper , to taste
  • 4 x 175g/6oz gurnard fillets, pin boned, skin scored
  • 110g/4oz streaky bacon, cut into lardons

Method

  1. Heat two tablespoons of the oil in a large saucepan and fry the onion and potatoes gently for about 10 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened but not brown.

  2. Add the garlic and cook for a further minute.

  3. Add the stock and season with salt, to taste. Bring the stew to the boil, then reduce the heat, cover and simmer for about 10 minutes or until the potatoes are tender.

  4. Add the broad beans and cook for a further minute. Stir in the chopped spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the spinach is wilted. Finally, stir in the mint and season with freshly ground black pepper, to taste.

  5. Heat a frying pan until hot, add the remaining olive oil and fry the gurnard fillets, skin side down, for 2-3 minutes, or until just crisp. Flip the fillets over and continue to cook for another two minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.

  6. Remove the gurnard from the pan and set aside to rest for 1-2 minutes.

  7. Meanwhile, heat a separate frying pan and fry the bacon lardons for 2-3 minutes, or until golden-brown and crisp.

  8. To serve, divide the stew between two bowls, top each with two gurnard fillets and sprinkle over the bacon lardons.
