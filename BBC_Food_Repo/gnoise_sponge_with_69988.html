Génoise sponge with blueberry compôte and vanilla cream

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 1 x 22cm/9in cake

Dietary

Vegetarian

This superbly light sponge cake makes a wonderful afternoon treat with a cuppa, or try it as a dessert served with lashings of cream.

By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the génoise sponge

  • 250g/9oz caster sugar
  • 8 medium free-range eggs
  • 250g/9oz plain flour
  • 50g/1¾oz butter , melted

For the blueberry compôte and vanilla cream

  • 250g/9oz blueberries
  • 100g/3½oz caster sugar
  • 500ml/18fl oz double cream
  • 1 vanilla pod , cut in half lengthways and seeds scraped out
  • icing sugar , for dusting

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4. Grease and line two 22cm/9in round cake tins.

  2. For the génoise sponge, place the sugar and eggs in a large bowl and whisk until thick and pale.

  3. Fold in the flour and melted butter and divide between the prepared cake tins.

  4. Bake in the oven for 20-30 minutes, or until the cakes are risen and springy to the touch. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool. When cool enough to handle, turn out on to a wire rack to cool completely.

  5. For the blueberry compôte and vanilla cream, place the blueberries, sugar and four tablespoons of water in a small saucepan. Place over a medium heat and simmer for five minutes. Remove from the heat and set aside to cool and thicken.

  6. Place the cream in a large bowl and add the vanilla seeds. Whisk until thick.

  7. To serve, spread the cooled compôte over the top of one cake and top with the whipped cream. Place the other cake on top and dust with icing sugar.
