Rhubarb omelette soufflé with vanilla cream and sarsaparilla

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 6

Dietary

Vegetarian

A light fluffy omelette soufflé with punchy rhubarb makes a perfect dinner party dessert. Cooked live at the National Festival of Making.

By Nigel Haworth

Ingredients

For the omelette soufflé

  • 6 free-range eggs, separated (you will only need 3 of the yolks)
  • 75g/2½oz granulated sugar
  • ½ lemon , zest only
  • 1 vanilla pod , seeds only
  • 40g/1½oz butter
  • 30g/1oz plain flour

For the rhubarb compôte

  • 300g/10½oz rhubarb , chopped
  • 50g/1¾oz caster sugar

For the vanilla cream

  • 200ml/7fl oz whipping or double cream
  • 100g/3½oz icing sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the sarsaparilla reduction

  • 400ml/14fl oz sarsaparilla Juice

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 190C/170C Fan/Gas 5.

  2. For the omelette soufflé, whisk 3 of the egg yolks in a bowl with 25g/1oz of the sugar, grated lemon zest and vanilla seeds scraped from the pod until pale and fluffy.

  3. Melt the butter in a small saucepan and set aside. Sift the flour into the egg yolk mixture and gently fold in along with the melted butter.

  4. Place the egg whites and another 25g/1oz of the sugar into bowl and beat with an electric whisk until frothy, then slowly add the rest of the sugar whisking until soft peaks are formed.

  5. Stir a third of the whisked egg whites into the egg yolk mixture. Gently fold the remainder of the egg whites in until fully incorporated.

  6. Divide the mixture equally between 6 omelette pans. Place the omelette pans onto a baking tray lined with a wet tea towel and bake for 13 minutes, until just set.

  7. Meanwhile, for the rhubarb compôte, place the rhubarb and sugar in a small saucepan cook over a low heat for about 15 minutes or until the rhubarb has broken down.

  8. For the vanilla cream, whip the cream with the sugar using a whisk until soft peaks are formed. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  9. For the sarsaparilla reduction, boil the sarsaparilla juice in a saucepan for 15–30 minutes, until it has reduced to a thick consistency.

  10. Serve the soufflés with the rhubarb compôte, vanilla cream and the sarsaparilla reduction drizzled over.
