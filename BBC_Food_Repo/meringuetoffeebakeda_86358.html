Meringue toffee baked Alaska with toffee sauce

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 8

Dietary

Vegetarian
By James Martin
From Saturday Kitchen Best Bites

Ingredients

For the baked Alaska

  • 50ml/2fl oz water
  • 225g/8oz white caster sugar
  • 4 free-range egg whites
  • 75g/3oz hazelnuts , finely chopped
  • 1 small ready-made chocolate sponge
  • 500ml/1lb 2oz vanilla ice cream
  • 500ml/1lb 2oz toffee ice cream

For the toffee sauce

  • 500ml/18fl oz double cream
  • 175g/6oz demerara sugar
  • 175g/6oz butter
  • 1 tbsp golden syrup
  • 1 tbsp black treacle

Method

  1. For the baked Alaska, place the water and sugar into a pan and bring to the boil. Skim the surface and wipe the sides of the pan with a wet pastry brush to remove any sugar crystals, then increase the heat so that the syrup boils rapidly.

  2. Insert a sugar thermometer. When the sugar mixture reaches 110C/225F beat the egg whites with an electric mixer until stiff peaks are formed.

  3. When the sugar mixture reaches 121C/250F remove from the heat. It will have formed a syrup.

  4. Turn the electric mixer onto the lowest setting then pour the sugar syrup into the egg whites in a thin, steady stream, continuing to beat until all the syrup is incorporated.

  5. Continue to beat at a slow speed until the mixture is almost cold, this can take up to 15 minutes.

  6. Fold the hazelnuts into the egg white mixture then spoon into a piping bag fitted with a plain nozzle.

  7. Place the chocolate sponge onto a serving plate and top with alternate scoops of vanilla and toffee ice cream.

  8. Pipe the meringue around the ice cream, covering completely from top to bottom. Glaze the meringue with a mini-blowtorch, or bake briefly in a very hot oven.

  9. For the toffee sauce, pour the cream into a pan and heat gently. Add the sugar and butter and whisk until melted. Whisk in the syrup and treacle.

  10. To serve, cut wedges of baked Alaska and place on serving plates. Drizzle the sauce over the top.
