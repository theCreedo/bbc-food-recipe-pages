Rudolph mince pies

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Makes 20 mince pies

These fun mince pies have a lovely almond filling and are topped with a glacé cherry – perfect for kids (or Santa!).

Equipment and preparation: You will need a food processor, circular pastry cutters and a 12-hole muffin tray

By Joanne Wheatley
From Christmas Kitchen

Ingredients

For the pastry

  • 250g/9oz plain flour , plus extra for dusting
  • 125g/4½oz cold unsalted butter , cut into cubes, plus extra for greasing
  • pinch sea salt
  • 2 large free-range egg yolks
  • 2-3 tbsp milk

For the filling

  • 55g/2oz unsalted butter
  • 50g/1¾oz granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp runny honey
  • 1 tbsp double cream
  • 85g/3oz flaked almonds
  • 1 x 210g jar good-quality mincemeat (or use homemade)
  • 10 glacé cherries , halved

Method

  1. For the pastry, put the flour, butter and salt into a food processor and pulse until you have a breadcrumb consistency. Add the egg yolks, pulse briefly, then add the milk a tablespoon at a time and pulse until the dough comes together. Turn out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead very quickly with your hands until the dough is smooth. Wrap in cling film and chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.

  2. For the filling, melt the butter, sugar and honey together in a heavy-bottomed saucepan until the sugar has dissolved. Bring to the boil and remove from the heat. Stir in the cream and almonds and leave to cool completely.

  3. Preheat the oven to 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6. Grease 2 x 12-hole muffin trays with butter.

  4. Remove the pastry from the fridge and roll out between two sheets of baking paper. Using a cutter, cut out 20 circles of pastry slightly larger than the top of the muffin tray holes. Line the muffin tray with the circles of pastry. Fill with a heaped teaspoon of mincemeat and top with the almond filling.

  5. Bake for 18-22 minutes, covering with foil when the filling turns golden-brown. Remove from the oven and immediately pop half a glacé cherry on each pie, cut-side down. Transfer to a wire rack and serve warm.
