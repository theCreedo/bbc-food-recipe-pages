Baklava bites

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 24

Dietary

Vegetarian

These sweet little parcels of ground pistachios are flavoured with a delicious honey and cinnamon syrup.

For this recipe you will need a canelle knife and a 24-hole mini muffin tin.

By Andrew Smyth
From The Great British Bake Off

Ingredients

For the filling

  • 140g/5oz pistachio kernels
  • 20g/¾oz caster sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon

For the pastry

  • 4 sheets readymade filo pastry
  • cornflour , for dusting
  • 75g/2½oz unsalted butter , melted

For the syrup

  • 120g/4oz clear honey
  • 25g/1oz caster sugar
  • ½ lime , zest only
  • 1 cinnamon stick

For the decoration

  • 2 limes , zest removed in long, thin strips using a canelle knife
  • 50g/1¾oz pistachio kernels, halved

Method

  1. For the filling, put the pistachios, sugar and cinnamon in a food processor and pulse until finely chopped (don’t to over chop as it will turn into a paste). Set the mixture aside in a bowl.

  2. Preheat the oven to 220C/200C Fan/Gas 7.

  3. For the pastry, lightly dust a work surface with cornflour and cut the sheets of filo into 24 x 5cm/2in squares.

  4. Take one square, dust off any excess cornflour and brush with melted butter. Set another square on top at a 45 degree angle and apply more butter. Transfer to a 24-hole mini muffin tin, gently pressing the pastry into a hole. Repeat to fill all the holes.

  5. Fill each pastry case with the filling, but don’t compress the mixture as this will prevent the syrup being absorbed properly later. Bake for about 14 minutes, or until the pastry is crisp and golden-brown.

  6. Meanwhile, make the syrup. Add the honey, sugar, lime zest, cinnamon and 55ml/2fl oz water to a small saucepan set over a medium heat. Bring to the boil then simmer until the syrup is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon (about 15 minutes). Remove from the heat and carefully strain the syrup (boiling sugar is very hot, so take care not to burn yourself) and immediately pour it over the hot baklava bites as they come out of the oven.

  7. For the decoration, place strips of lime zest and a pistachio on each baklava bite. Serve warm or cold.
