Fried fish with garlic and spring onions

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Strips of battered fish, shallow fried and served with a hot sauce. Perfect for eating with Singapore noodles.

By Ken Hom
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 450g/1lb fillets of sole or plaice (dark skin removed), cut into 2.5cm/1in strips
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground white pepper
  • 2 medium free-range eggs
  • 50g/1¾oz cornflour
  • 150ml/5fl oz groundnut or vegetable oil
  • 3 tbsp garlic , finely sliced
  • 2 tbsp spring onions , finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine or dry sherry
  • 2 tsp sesame oil

Method

  1. If you are using plaice, ask the fishmonger to remove the dark skin of the plaice or remove it yourself with a small, sharp knife.

  2. Cut the fish fillets into 2.5cm/1in strips. Sprinkle them with a teaspoon of salt and the pepper. Set aside in the fridge for 15 minutes.

  3. Beat the eggs in a small bowl. Coat the pieces of fish in cornflour and then beaten egg.

  4. Heat a wok or large frying-pan over high heat until hot. Add the oil. When it is very hot and slightly smoking, turn the heat down to medium. Shallow-fry the fish pieces on each side, in several batches, until they are golden-brown. Drain them on kitchen paper. Pour off the oil and discard it.

  5. Wipe the wok or pan clean. Add the rest of the ingredients and the remaining teaspoon of salt. Simmer for 2 minutes. Put the fried fish fillets on a warm serving platter and pour over the hot sauce. Serve at once.
