Sesame tuna with noodles

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Fresh tuna is such a treat, especially when it's given the sweet and tangy Chinese style treatment.

By Simon Rimmer
From Something for the Weekend

Ingredients

  • 800g/1lb 12¼oz loin of tuna (the piece should be cylinder-shaped)
  • 1 free-range egg white , lightly beaten
  • 100g/3½oz sesame seeds
  • 100g/3½oz poppy seeds
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • dash vegetable oil , to fry

For the crisp-fried noodles (optional)

  • vegetable oil , for deep frying
  • small handful dried fine egg noodles

For the dressing

  • 125ml/4½fl oz light soy sauce
  • 2.5cm/1in piece fresh ginger
  • 200ml/7¼oz clear honey
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 lime , juice only

For the vegetables

  • 1 bok choi , quartered, blanched in boiling water and refreshed in ice cold water
  • 100g/3½oz broccoli , cut into very small florets
  • 1 carrot , finely cut into matchstick-sized pieces
  • 2 spring onions , sliced
  • 1 red pepper , finely sliced
  • dash vegetable oil , to fry

Method

  1. Brush the tuna with the egg white. Scatter the seeds over a clean work surface or flat plate and roll the tuna in the seeds until covered all over. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste.

  2. Wrap the tuna very tightly in cling film to make a solid barrel shape. Transfer to the fridge and chill for two hours.

  3. For the crisp-fried noodles (if you are making them), heat the vegetable oil in a deep heavy-bottomed saucepan until a breadcrumb sizzles and turns golden-browns when dropped in it. (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous - do not leave unattended.)

  4. Deep fry the egg noodles for 1-2 minutes until crisp, remove with a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper.

  5. For the dressing, blend the soy sauce, ginger, honey, sesame oil and lime juice together in a blender or food processor until smooth.

  6. When you are ready to cook the tuna and the vegetables, heat both a pan and a wok until smoking.

  7. Add vegetable oil to both the pan and the wok, place the tuna in the pan and cook for about 30 seconds on all sides.

  8. For the vegetables, add all the vegetables to the wok, mix well and then add some of the dressing.

  9. Serve the tuna on the vegetables, drizzled with the remaining dressing and topped with the crisp-fried noodles.
