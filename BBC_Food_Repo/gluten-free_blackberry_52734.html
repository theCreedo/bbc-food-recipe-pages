Gluten-free blackberry and ginger cheesecake

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 9

To make these gluten-free cheesecakes Christmassy, Howard made cute gingerbread houses with a delicious cheesecake garden.

By Howard Middleton
From The Great British Bake Off

Ingredients

For the cheesecake bases

  • 150g/5½oz gluten-free ginger biscuits, finely crushed
  • 30g/1oz unsalted butter , melted

For the cheesecake filling

  • 400g/14oz soft curd cheese
  • 100g/3½oz quark
  • 2 large free-range eggs
  • 4 tbsp green ginger wine
  • 4 tbsp maple syrup
  • 4 pieces preserved stem ginger , finely chopped

For the blackberry coulis

  • 300g/10½oz fresh blackberries (or frozen, defrosted)
  • 30g/1oz caster sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract or vanilla bean paste

For the decoration

  • 9 blackberries
  • 9 gluten-free gingerbread biscuits , cut into house shapes
  • icing sugar , for dusting

Method

  1. For the cheesecake bases, mix the crushed biscuits with the melted butter. Press this firmly into a 20cm/8in square baking tin and pop it into the freezer while you make the filling.

  2. To make the filling, whisk the curd cheese, quark, eggs, green ginger wine and maple syrup together until smooth and light.

  3. Remove the biscuit base from the freezer and sprinkle over the chopped stem ginger. Pour the filling on top and bake for 35-40 minutes until just set. Leave to cool in the tin and then chill until you’re ready to assemble.

  4. Make a blackberry coulis by putting the blackberries, sugar and 5 tablespoons water in a small pan. Bring to the boil, then simmer for 5 minutes. Stir in the vanilla, blend with a stick blender and strain through a sieve. Leave to cool then chill until needed.

  5. To serve, cut the cheesecake into 9 squares. Lift carefully out of the tin onto a serving plate. Press a biscuit house upright against each cheesecake slice firmly. Place a blackberry on top of the cheesecake and dust with icing sugar. Serve with the blackberry coulis.
