Singapore fried noodles with spiced duck breast

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 1

By Paul Rankin
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the duck breast

  • ½ tbsp coriander seeds
  • ½ tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ duck breast, fat scored with a sharp knife
  • ½ tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey

For the noodles

  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
  • 3 shallots , finely sliced
  • 150g/5½oz rice noodles , blanched
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp chilli , finely chopped
  • ½ tsp mild curry powder
  • 1 free-range egg
  • 1 tsp fresh chives , chopped

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. For the duck, grind the coriander and black peppercorns in a pestle and mortar and rub the spice rub over the duck breast.

  3. Season the duck breast with salt and place skin-side down into a small, ovenproof frying pan over a medium heat. Fry the breast for five minutes, until the skin is browned. Drain off the released duck fat and turn the breast.

  4. Add the soy sauce and honey and fry for one minute, then transfer to the oven for 5 minutes, or until cooked through. Remove from the oven and rest the duck breast for one minute.

  5. Thinly slice the breast.

  6. To make the noodles, place the oil in a non-stick wok over a high heat.

  7. Add the shallot and stir fry for one minute.

  8. Add the noodles and stir fry for one minute.

  9. Add the soy sauce, chilli and curry powder and stir fry for one minute.

  10. Add the egg and stir fry for one minute, until cooked through.

  11. To serve, place the noodles onto a plate, place the sliced duck breast on top and sprinkle with chives.
