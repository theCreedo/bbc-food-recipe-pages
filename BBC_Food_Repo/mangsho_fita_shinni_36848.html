Mangsho fita shinni (beef curry)

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

1 to 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4-6

Nadiya's beef curry sings of spice, serve it with crunchy okra for a taste of Bangladesh.

By Nadiya Hussain
From The Chronicles of Nadiya

Ingredients

For the beef curry

  • 5 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large cinnamon stick
  • 5 cardamom pods
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 5cm/2in piece fresh root ginger , finely chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 large onion , chopped
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 500g/1lb 2oz beef , boneless, cubed
  • 3 chillies , sliced lengthways (remove seeds if you want less heat)
  • handful fresh coriander
  • salt

For the fita

  • 250g/9oz rice flour , plus extra for dusting
  • pinch salt

For the okra

  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 300g/10½oz okra , halved lengthwise
  • 4 tbsp vegetable oil , for frying

Method

  1. For the curry, heat the oil in a large pan. Add the cinnamon, cardamom and bay leaves and cook for 1 minute. Add the ginger, garlic, onion and cook for 10 minutes. Add the ground spices and cook for 1 minute, taking care not to burn them. Add the beef and cook over a medium-high heat for 30 minutes.

  2. Add 200ml/7fl oz water, season with salt. Cook over a medium heat for 30 minutes. Remove from the heat and add the chillies and coriander (reserve some coriander for garnish).

  3. Meanwhile, for the fita, bring 750ml/26fl oz water to the boil in a large pan with a little salt. Remove from the heat and add the rice flour. Leave for 10 minutes, covered, to cook the rice out. Remove the dough while still hot and roll out onto a surface dusted with rice flour. Cut out into small rounds.

  4. Heat a flat pan over a medium heat. Cook the fita for about 1 minute on each side, until they are cooked through but have not coloured.

  5. For the okra, work the paprika, garlic powder, chilli flakes and ½ teaspoon salt into the okra. Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over a high heat. Briefly fry the okra – it should keep its colour and have a soft bite to it.

  6. To serve, spoon out the beef curry and serve with the fita and the okra. Garnish with the coriander.
