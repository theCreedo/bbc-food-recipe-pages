Caramelised butternut squash tart with pine nut pastry and chive yoghurt

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 1

Dietary

Vegetarian
By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the caramelised butternut squash

  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 55g/2oz butter
  • 150g/5½oz butternut squash , peeled and roughly chopped

For the pine nut pastry

  • 100g/3½oz pine nuts , crushed
  • 100g/3½oz plain flour , plus extra for dusting
  • 85g/3oz cold butter , cut into cubes
  • 1 free-range egg , beaten
  • splash milk

To serve

  • 2 tbsp Greek-style yoghurt
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh chives

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/250F/Gas 4.

  2. For the caramelised butternut squash, place the sugar into a small ovenproof frying pan and heat gently until melted and caramel-coloured. Add the butter and stir to combine.

  3. Add the butternut squash pieces, stir well and cook gently for 3-4 minutes, until softened.

  4. For the pastry, place the crushed pine nuts, plain flour and cold butter into a food processor and pulse until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.

  5. Add the beaten egg and a splash of milk if the mixture is still too dry and blend to combine.

  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and roll out to the thickness of a pound coin.

  7. Place the pastry over the cooked butternut squash in the pan, pressing down around the edges to enclose the filling and trimming off any excess pastry.

  8. Transfer to the oven and bake for 12-14 minutes, or until golden-brown.

  9. Remove the tart from the oven and allow to cool slightly. Slide a knife around the edges of the frying pan and turn the tart out onto a serving plate.

  10. To serve, mix the yoghurt and chives together in a bowl and place a dollop of chive yoghurt on top of the tart.
