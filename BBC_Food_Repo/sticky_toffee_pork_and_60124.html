Sticky toffee pork and vinegar slaw

Preparation time

30 mins to 1 hour

Cooking time

over 2 hours

Serves

Serves 4

Treat to yourself to something really special, from meat chef guru Richard H. Turner.

By Richard H Turner
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the master pork broth

  • 2 onions , halved
  • 1kg/2lb 4oz pork bones, lightly roasted
  • 2 pork hocks
  • 2 pig’s feet
  • 1 smoked gammon or bacon hock
  • 2 large carrots , halved
  • 2 celery sticks
  • 2 large dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 bouquet garni , made with thyme, bay, rosemary and parsley
  • 1 spice bag, made with 20 fennel seeds, 1 star anise, 2 cloves, ½ cinnamon stick, 1 small knob of fresh root ginger
  • 250ml/9fl oz Madeira
  • 250ml/9fl oz soy sauce

For the pork

  • 1kg/2lb 4oz pork belly , bone in
  • 20g/¾oz sea salt flakes
  • 2 litres of master pork broth (from above), or chicken broth
  • ½ tsp Chinese five-spice powder
  • 2 fresh chillies , chopped
  • ¼ tsp yellow mustard seeds
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp black pepper corns
  • 100ml/3½fl oz blackstrap molasses
  • 100ml/3½fl oz light soy sauce
  • 100g/3½oz dates , stoned and chopped
  • 100g/3½oz light brown muscovado sugar

For the dressing

  • 50ml/2fl oz cider vinegar
  • 50ml/2fl oz white wine vinegar
  • 5 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 35g/1oz caster sugar

For the vinegar slaw

  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 200g/7oz white cabbage
  • 100g/3½oz red cabbage
  • 100g/3½oz fennel
  • 1 red onion
  • handful mixed fresh herbs (parsley, coriander and mint)
  • 1 tsp sea salt flakes
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 unwaxed lemon , juice and zest

Method

  1. To make the master pork broth, blacken the cut side of the onions in a very hot, dry pan. Place in a large pan with all the other ingredients and 5 litres/8 ¾ pints of water. Bring to a gentle simmer. Cook for 3 hours, skimming every 30 minutes or so. The trick here is to simmer at a bare roll and skim any impurities regularly, to achieve a clean, clear broth.

  2. Without moving the pan, turn off the heat and gently ladle the broth out of the pot through a very fine sieve, taking care not to disturb the bottom too much. Cool and reserve the master pork broth until needed. (You can use the meats and vegetables to make pork broth salad or pork broth terrine.)

  3. For the pork, generously salt the pork belly and leave uncovered for 1 hour, then rinse. Place in a large saucepan and cover with the pork broth. Add the rest of the ingredients.

  4. Bring to the boil, then turn down to a simmer. Place a plate or a small saucepan lid directly on the pork belly to keep it submerged. Cook at the barest simmer for about 2 hours. When you can pass a skewer through the belly with ease, it is ready.

  5. Meanwhile preheat the oven to 220C/200C Fan/Gas 7.

  6. Take care when removing the pork belly from the stock. Place it on a chopping board and remove the bones and sinews (they should pop out of the tender meat).

  7. Score the skin slightly in a chequered pattern. Place on greaseproof paper on a baking tray and roast for about 20 minutes.

  8. Meanwhile, strain the reserved stock into a saucepan and reduce it until a nice coating consistency is achieved, like a light syrup.

  9. For the dressing, whisk all the dressing ingredients together until the sugar has dissolved, and store in a sealed container.

  10. For the slaw, toast the fennel seeds in a dry pan until golden-brown, then set aside.

  11. Slice the cabbage, fennel and onion 1 mm thick, using a mandolin – the thinner the better. Finely chop the herbs using a very sharp knife.

  12. Toss together with all the remaining ingredients so that they are well combined and the herbs are evenly spread throughout the slaw.

  13. Whisk the dressing again 20 minutes before serving and add to the slaw. Toss thoroughly and check and adjust the seasoning.

  14. Serve the pork with the sauce drizzled over it and the slaw on the side.
