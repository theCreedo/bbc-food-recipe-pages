Blue jewel bake

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Makes 6

Dietary

Vegetarian

These bite-sized muffins are perfect for picnics, lunchboxes and kids' parties.

By Neil Strawson and Richard Hunt
From I Can Cook on the Go

Ingredients

  • vegetable oil , for greasing
  • 1 free-range egg
  • 100ml/3½fl oz milk
  • 1 tsp runny honey
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp orange zest (or ½ tsp orange extract)
  • 45g/1¾oz self-raising flour
  • 30 blueberries
  • plain yoghurt , to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 220C/450F/Gas 7. Grease a six-hole muffin tin with vegetable oil using a pastry brush.

  2. Beat the egg together with the milk in a jug until well combined. Add the honey, vanilla extract and orange zest and beat again.

  3. Put the flour into a mixing bowl and make a well in the centre. Pour in half of the egg mixture, gradually bringing the edges of the flour into the wet mixture with a fork until the mixture is smooth and thoroughly combined.

  4. Pour over the remaining egg mixture and mix again with the fork to form a smooth, runny batter.

  5. Divide the blueberries equally among the six holes in the muffin tray. Spoon in the batter (about three tablespoons of batter per hole).

  6. Bake the muffins in the oven for 10-15 minutes, or until they have risen and are golden-brown, and a skewer inserted into the centre of each comes out clean.

  7. Set aside to cool slightly, then remove the muffins from the tray and set aside to cool on a wire rack. These are delicious eaten as they are, or served with plain yoghurt.
