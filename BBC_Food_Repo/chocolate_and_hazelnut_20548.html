Chocolate and hazelnut éclairs

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Makes 16 éclairs

Dietary

Vegetarian

Better than any deli offering, these indulgent pastries make an impressive afternoon treat.

Ingredients

For the éclairs

  • 125g/4oz butter
  • 125ml/4fl oz water
  • 125ml/4fl oz milk
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 150g/5oz plain flour
  • 4 free-range eggs , beaten

For the hazelnut filling

  • 150ml/5fl oz whipping cream
  • 1 tbsp instant coffee mixed with 1 tbsp boiling water, cooled
  • 50g/2oz hazelnuts , toasted, chopped

For the topping

  • 100g/3½fl oz milk chocolate

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6. Line a baking tray with greaseproof paper.

  2. For the éclairs, heat the butter, water, milk and sugar in a saucepan until the mixture is boiling. Remove the pan from the heat and beat in the flour all at once, until the mixture comes together as a dough. Set aside to cool slightly then beat in the eggs a little at a time.

  3. Spoon the mixture into a piping bag and pipe 16 éclairs onto a baking tray. Bake in the oven for 10 minutes, then reduce the oven temperature to 180C/350F/Gas 4 and bake for a further 7-8 minutes.

  4. Remove the éclairs from oven and pierce a hole in each to let out steam. Return to the oven for a further 5 minutes, then set aside to cool on a wire rack.

  5. Meanwhile, for the hazelnut filling, whip the double cream in a bowl until soft peaks form when the whisk is removed. Stir in the coffee and half of the toasted hazelnuts. Chill in the fridge until needed.

  6. For the topping, melt the chocolate in a bowl set over a saucepan of simmering water (do not let the base of the bowl touch the water).

  7. When the éclairs have cooled, slice them in half and fill with the hazelnut filling.

  8. Drizzle the melted chocolate over the éclairs and sprinkle with the remaining toasted hazelnuts. Chill in the fridge for 10 minutes. Serve chilled.
