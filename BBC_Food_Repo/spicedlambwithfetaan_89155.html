Spiced lamb with feta and chargrilled Baby Gem salad and lemon pilaf

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

By Maria Elia
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the spiced lamb

  • 200g/7oz canon of lamb
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • dash olive oil

For the feta and chargrilled Baby Gem salad

  • 1 Baby Gem lettuce , leaves separated
  • 1 tbsp olive oil , plus extra for drizzling
  • 100g/3½oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 lemon , zest and juice only
  • 1 tbsp Kalamata olives , stones removed, chopped
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the lemon pilaf

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion , finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic , finely chopped
  • 1 lemon , zest only
  • 150g/5½oz long grain rice
  • 300ml/10½fl oz hot chicken stock
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh mint

Method

  1. For the lamb, rub the lamb with the cinnamon and oregano and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Heat a griddle pan until smoking, rub the lamb with the olive oil and fry for 3-4 minutes on each side (for rare), or until golden-brown all over and cooked to your liking. Remove from the pan and leave to rest for five minutes, then cut into thick slices.

  2. For the feta and Baby Gem salad, toss the Baby Gem lettuce leaves with the olive oil. Heat a griddle pan until smoking, then add the lettuce and fry for 15-20 seconds on each side, or until tender. Mix the chargrilled lettuce together with the feta, lemon zest and juice and olives. Drizzle over some olive oil, season with salt and freshly ground black pepper and stir well.

  3. For the lemon pilaf, heat the olive oil in a saucepan, add the onion and garlic and fry for 4-5 five minutes, or until softened but not coloured. Add the lemon zest and the rice, stirring to coat in the oil. Gradually add the hot chicken stock and cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring frequently, or until all of the stock has been absorbed and the rice is tender. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper and stir in the chopped mint.

  4. To serve, place the salad onto two serving plates, top each with the sliced lamb and serve the lemon pilaf on the side.
