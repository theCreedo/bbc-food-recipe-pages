Smoked salmon and cream cheese potato pancakes

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

less than 10 mins

Serves

Serves 2

By Tony Tobin
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the pancakes

  • 110g/4oz self-raising flour
  • pinch salt
  • 1 free-range egg , beaten
  • 1 free-range egg yolk
  • 150ml/¼ pint milk
  • 1 spring onion , chopped
  • 6 new potatoes , peeled and grated
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the cream cheese topping

  • 100g/3½oz cream cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh chives
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley

To serve

  • 2 slices smoked salmon
  • few sprigs fresh dill

Method

  1. For the pancakes, place the flour into a large bowl, add the salt, the beaten egg and the egg yolk and mix well. Gradually whisk in the milk to make a smooth batter.

  2. Add the spring onions and grated potatoes to the batter and stir well.

  3. Heat half of the olive oil in a frying pan and remove the excess with kitchen paper. Pour in spoonfuls of the batter and fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden-brown on both sides. Remove the pancakes from the pan and keep warm. Repeat the process with the remaining oil and batter.

  4. For the cream cheese topping, place the cream cheese, chives and parsley into a small bowl and mix well.

  5. To serve, place the pancakes onto serving plates and top with the cream cheese mixture and the salmon slices. Garnish with fresh dill.
