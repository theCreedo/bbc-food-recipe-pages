Smoked salmon stack with cured egg yolk

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

Plan in this dish - you need a couple of days to cure the egg. They can sit happily in the back of your fridge for four days and the final dish takes minutes to assemble.

By Andi Oliver
From Christmas Kitchen

Ingredients

For the cured egg yolk

  • 100g/3½oz sea salt
  • 100g/3½oz caster sugar
  • 1 vanilla pod , seeds only
  • pinch dried rose petals
  • 2 medium free-range egg yolks

For the smoked salmon stack

  • ½ avocado , chopped
  • 2 spring onions , finely chopped
  • 1 tsp finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tsp finely snipped fresh chives
  • 1 lime , zest and juice
  • 2 tsp rapeseed oil
  • 50g/1¾oz smoked salmon , chopped
  • ½ red onion , finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tsp capers
  • 1 tsp cress

For the latke

  • 1 potato , grated
  • ¼ onion , grated
  • 1 free-range egg , beaten
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Method

  1. For the dry-cure, put the salt, sugar, vanilla seeds and rose petals in a food processor, then blend to a powder. Tip half the mixture onto a small baking tray, make two little wells in the mixture and carefully drop in the yolks. Tip over the remaining dry-cure, cover with cling film and chill in the fridge for 4 days.

  2. For the smoked salmon stack, combine the avocado, spring onion, parsley, chives, lime zest, half the rapeseed oil and half the lime juice in a bowl. Position two 7.5cm/3in chefs’ rings onto serving plates. Spoon the avocado mixture into the centre of the moulds.

  3. Combine the salmon and red onion with the remaining rapeseed oil and lime juice. Spoon this on top of the avocado mixture.

  4. Rinse the dry-cure off the egg yolks, pat dry and grate into a bowl. Add the capers and mix gently. Spoon the mixture on top of the salmon and scatter with the cress.

  5. For the latke, put the grated potato in a clean tea towel and squeeze out any excess liquid. Put the potato in a bowl and mix it with the grated onion and egg. Heat the oil in a frying pan placed over a medium heat. Divide the potato mixture into two cakes and fry for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until crispy on both sides and cooked through.

  6. Carefully lift up the ring moulds, releasing the salmon stacks, and serve with the latke.
