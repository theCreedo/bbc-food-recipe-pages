Lambs’ liver and sweetbread with pickled spring onions and samphire

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 4

Lambs’ liver is great value and tasty too. Try it in this simple recipe served with Jersey Royals.

By Richard Corrigan
From 2007-16

Ingredients

  • 75g/3oz plain flour, seasoned with salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 600g/1lb 5oz lambs’ liver , cut into thick slices
  • 50g/2oz butter , plus extra for the potatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 8 lamb sweetbreads
  • 150ml/5fl oz white wine vinegar
  • 200g/7oz caster sugar
  • 3 tbsp mustard seeds , crushed
  • 2 bunches spring onions , trimmed
  • 150g/5oz samphire
  • 250g/9oz Jersey Royal potatoes
  • 2 sprigs fresh mint , leaves picked and finely sliced
  • 1 bunch fresh dill , chopped

Method

  1. Sprinkle the flour onto a plate and dredge the lambs’ liver in the flour.

  2. Heat a frying pan until hot, add the butter, oil, lambs’ liver and sweetbreads and fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the lambs’ liver is golden-brown.

  3. Remove the lambs’ liver and set aside to rest, then cook the sweetbreads for a further 2-3 minutes, or until golden-brown and crisp.

  4. Meanwhile, heat the vinegar and sugar in a saucepan until boiling, then add the crushed mustard seeds.

  5. Slice off the white part of the spring onions, add to the saucepan and poach gently for 2-3 minutes, or until just tender. Remove the pan from the heat.

  6. Slice the remaining green part to the onion finely and set aside.

  7. Cook the Jersey Royals in a pan of boiling, salted water for 5-7 minutes, or until just tender, then drain.

  8. Return the Jersey Royals to the saucepan with a little butter, salt, freshly ground black pepper and the mint, and stir until well combined.

  9. Blanch the samphire in a pan of salted boiling water for 1-2 minutes, or until hot through. Drain.

  10. To serve, remove the poached spring onion from the saucepan and add to the samphire, then stir in a little of the poaching liquid.

  11. Arrange the samphire on serving plates with the lambs’ liver and sweetbreads. Stir the dill and sliced spring onion into the remaining poaching liquid and spoon over the plates. Serve the Jersey Royals alongside.
