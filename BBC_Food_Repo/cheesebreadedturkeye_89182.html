Cheese-breaded turkey escalope with fried potatoes and sweet pepper sauce

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 1

By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the cheese-breaded turkey escalope

  • 1 slice white bread , crusts removed
  • 1 small handful fresh basil leaves
  • 55g/2oz mature cheddar , grated
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 turkey escalope
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 free-range egg , beaten
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 25g/1oz butter

For the fried new potatoes

  • 25g/1oz butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 150g/5½oz new potatoes , cooked, sliced
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the sweet pepper sauce

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 red pepper , finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic , chopped
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 25g/1oz butter
  • splash water
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley , to garnish

Method

  1. For the breaded turkey, place the bread, basil, cheddar, salt and freshly ground black pepper into a mini food processor and blend to fine breadcrumbs.

  2. Dredge the turkey escalope in the plain flour and shake off the excess, then dip into the beaten egg and finally into the breadcrumb mix. Pat gently to secure the breadcrumbs to the escalope, then shake off any excess.

  3. Heat the olive oil and butter in a frying pan, add the turkey escalope and fry for 3-4 minutes on both sides, or until golden-brown all over and cooked through.

  4. For the fried new potatoes, melt the butter and oil in a frying pan, add the potatoes and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Fry for 2-3 minutes on both sides, or until golden-brown on all sides.

  5. For the sweet pepper sauce, heat the olive oil in a saucepan, add the pepper and garlic and lightly fry for 3-4 minutes, or until the pepper has softened. Stir in the honey, butter and a splash of water, then transfer to a mini food processor and blend until smooth.

  6. To serve, pile the fried new potatoes onto a serving plate, top with the breaded turkey escalope and drizzle around the sweet pepper sauce. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and serve.
