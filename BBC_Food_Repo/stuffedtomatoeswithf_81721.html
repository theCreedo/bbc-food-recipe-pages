Stuffed tomatoes with fried parsnip and potato skins

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

By Paul Rankin
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the stuffed tomatoes

  • ¼ tsp coriander seeds
  • ¼ tsp cumin seeds
  • pinch chilli flakes
  • pinch ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic , finely chopped
  • ¼ onion , finely chopped
  • 100g/7oz minced beef
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tomatoes, halved and seeded

For the fried parsnip and potato skins

  • 200ml/7fl oz vegetable oil
  • 1 potato , skin (peelings) only
  • 1 parsnip , skin (peelings) only

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. For the stuffed tomatoes, place the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, chilli flakes and ginger in a pestle and mortar and grind to a powder.

  3. Add the ground spices and chilli flakes to a hot frying pan and dry fry for one minute to release their fragrance.

  4. Add the oil, garlic, onion and minced beef and fry for 4-5 minutes, or until browned. Season, to taste, with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Spoon the minced beef mixture into the hollow tomato halves, place onto a baking sheet, then place into the oven to bake for eight minutes.

  5. For the fried parsnip and potato skins, heat the vegetable oil in a deep, heavy bottomed saucepan until a breadcrumb sizzles and turns brown when added to it. (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous, do not leave unattended.) Carefully add the potato and parsnip skins and deep-fry for 3-4 minutes, until golden. Carefully remove and drain on kitchen towels.

  6. To serve, place the stuffed tomatoes onto a large plate and arrange the fried parsnip and potato skins around the plate.
