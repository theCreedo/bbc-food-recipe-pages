Paupiette of sole with wild mushrooms and herb sauce

Preparation time

1-2 hours

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 3

This elegant dish is perfect for a special supper.

By Frederick Forster
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the salmon mousse

  • 200g/7oz salmon fillets, skinned and pin-boned, cut into pieces
  • pinch salt
  • 1 small free-range egg white
  • 155ml/5fl oz double cream
  • pinch cayenne pepper
  • ½ unwaxed lemon , finely grated zest only
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh chives
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh flatleaf parsley

For the sole

  • 3 fillets Dover sole , pin-boned
  • 250ml/9fl oz fish stock
  • 50ml/2fl oz dry white wine
  • ½ shallot , finely chopped
  • knob of butter
  • 50g/1¾oz wild mushrooms , preferably chanterelles, torn up if large
  • micro watercress
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the citrus herb sauce

  • 3 tbsp double cream
  • 80g/2¾oz unsalted butter , cut into pieces
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh flatleaf parsley
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh chives
  • lemon juice , to taste

Method

  1. Blend the salmon with the salt in a food processor for 1–2 minutes, scrapping down the sides often. Add the egg white and blend for 20 seconds. Add half the cream gradually, blending all the time, until you have a smooth mousse. Put in a bowl and place over ice. Mix in the remaining cream, cayenne, lemon zest and herbs. Refrigerate for 10 minutes.

  2. Flatten the sole fillets slightly. Score the flesh side several times using a sharp knife. Season and spread the mousse all over, then roll up and secure with toothpicks. Refrigerate for at least an hour.

  3. Preheat the oven to 160C/140C Fan/Gas 3.

  4. Put the fish rolls in an ovenproof pan with the stock, wine and shallot. Bring to a gentle simmer, then cover with a lid or buttered greaseproof paper. Bake for 7–8 minutes, remove and leave to rest in a warm place for 4–5 minutes.

  5. For the sauce, strain the cooking liquid into a clean pan and cook over a high heat until reduced to a sauce consistency. Add the double cream and gradually whisk in the butter. Stir in the herbs and lemon juice to taste. Keep warm.

  6. Heat a knob of butter in a frying pan. When foaming, add the mushrooms and cook quickly, then drain.

  7. Divide the sole fillets between warm plates, scatter over the mushrooms and spoon over the sauce. Top with the watercress and serve.
