Apple and Cheshire cheese bake

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 1

Dietary

Vegetarian
By Brian Turner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

  • 1 apple
  • 30g/1oz butter
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 free-range egg whites , whisked until stiff
  • 50g/1¾oz Cheshire cheese, grated

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. Slice the top off the apple, chop the flesh from this part and place it into a bowl, discarding the rest of the top part. Then cut out as much flesh as possible from the main part of the apple, leaving the outer shell intact. Discard the core, chop up the flesh and add this to the bowl.

  3. Melt half of the butter in a small ovenproof frying pan, place the apple flesh into the pan and cook gently for 4-5 minutes, or until softened to a purée.

  4. Transfer the apple purée to a bowl and leave to cool for five minutes.

  5. Melt the remaining butter in the pan. Place the apple shell into the pan, sprinkle brown sugar over it and transfer to the oven to bake for 5-6 minutes, until beginning to soften and turn golden-brown.

  6. Meanwhile, place the whisked egg whites into the bowl with the apple purée and fold together, then fold in the Cheshire cheese.

  7. Remove the apple from the oven, spoon the purée mixture inside it and return to the oven for 5-6 minutes, or until puffed up and golden-brown.

  8. To serve, transfer the baked apple to a serving plate.
