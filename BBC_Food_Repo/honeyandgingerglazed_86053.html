Honey and ginger-glazed ribs with pak choi tempura and mango salsa

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

By James Tanner
From Ready Steady Cook

Ingredients

For the ribs

  • 1 x 3-rib pork rack
  • water, to cover
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme
  • 1 garlic clove, unpeeled

For the glaze

  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2.5cm/1in piece root ginger , peeled and grated
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 orange , juice only
  • 1 red chilli , chopped
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds , for sprinkling

For the pak choi tempura

  • 300ml/½ pint vegetable oil , for deep frying
  • small handful ice
  • 150g/5½oz self-raising flour
  • 85ml/3fl oz sparkling water
  • 2 small bulbs pak choi

For the mango salsa

  • ½ mango , peeled, stone removed, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh coriander , plus extra, to garnish
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200C/400F/Gas 6.

  2. For the ribs, place the ribs into a small saucepan and cover with boiling water. Add the rosemary, thyme and unpeeled garlic clove and bring to the boil. Boil for ten minutes, then drain and discard the water and herbs.

  3. For the glaze, place the sesame oil, honey, ginger, soy sauce, orange juice and chilli into a small pan and simmer for five minutes, or until reduced and sticky.

  4. Place the blanched ribs onto a baking sheet and brush with the glaze.

  5. Transfer to the oven and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the pork is completely cooked through.

  6. Place the ribs onto a serving plate, pour over any remaining glaze and sprinkle with the sesame seeds.

  7. Meanwhile, for the pak choi tempura, place the vegetable oil into a deep, heavy-bottomed saucepan and heat until a small cube of bread sizzles and turns golden when dropped into it. (CAUTION: hot oil can be dangerous. Do not leave unattended.)

  8. Place the ice and flour into a large bowl and gradually whisk in the sparkling water to make a batter the consistency of double cream.

  9. Dip the pak choi into the batter to coat, shaking off any excess, then carefully place into the hot oil. Deep fry for 4-5 minutes, or until crisp and golden-brown. Remove from the oil with a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper.

  10. For the mango salsa, place all of the mango salsa ingredients into a small bowl and stir to combine.

  11. To serve, place the pak choi tempura onto the plate with the ribs. Sprinkle over the coriander and serve the mango salsa in a bowl alongside.
