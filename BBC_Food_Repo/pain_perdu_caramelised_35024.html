Pain perdu, caramelised pineapple and candy floss

Preparation time

less than 30 mins

Cooking time

10 to 30 mins

Serves

Serves 2

Turn eggy bread into a fun dessert. Brioche slices are flavoured with vanilla and rum, fried, then topped with flamed pineapple and whipped cream.

By Glynn Purnell
From Saturday Kitchen

Ingredients

For the pain perdu and pineapple

  • 2 free-range eggs
  • 30g/1oz caster sugar
  • 250ml/9fl oz milk
  • 1 vanilla pod , split in half lengthways and seeds scraped out
  • 2 tbsp rum , plus extra to flambé
  • 2 thick slices brioche
  • 100g/3½oz brown sugar
  • ½ pineapple , peeled and cut into cubes
  • 60g/2¼oz butter

For the topping

  • 200ml/7fl oz whipping cream
  • 50g/1¾oz icing sugar
  • 1 vanilla pod , split in half lengthways and seeds scraped out
  • candy floss (optional), to serve

Method

  1. For the pain perdu and pineapple, mix the eggs, caster sugar, milk, vanilla and rum in a large bowl. Add the brioche and leave to soak.

  2. Heat the sugar in a pan until melted and slightly coloured. Add the pineapple and a little extra rum. Allow to heat briefly, then slowly tilt the pan towards the flame, or light with a match. Let the flames flare up then die down (CAUTION: be careful of flames and keep your face away from the pan). Add the butter and cook for 2 minutes.

  3. For the topping, whip the cream, icing sugar and vanilla together until soft peaks form when the whisk is removed.

  4. Place the soaked brioche in a hot frying pan and cook for 2 minutes on each side until golden-brown.

  5. Put the brioche in serving dishes, cover with the caramelised pineapple, add a spoonful of the whipped cream and cover with candy floss.
