Clementine rumtopf

Preparation time

overnight

Cooking time

30 mins to 1 hour

Serves

Serves 4

Dietary

Vegetarian

Fruit soaked in rum syrup for a week is a German Christmas tradition. It's served here with a simple upside-down cake.

By Matt Tebbutt
From Christmas Kitchen

Ingredients

For the rumtopf

  • 150g/5½oz caster sugar
  • 100ml/3½fl oz dark rum
  • 300–400g/10½–14oz clementine segments

For the upside-down cake

  • 100g/3½oz light soft brown sugar
  • 300g/10½oz unsalted butter , softened
  • 4 clementines , peeled and halved
  • 200g/7oz caster sugar
  • 200g/7oz self-raising flour
  • 1 vanilla pod , seeds removed, or 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 free-range eggs

To serve

  • vanilla ice cream

Method

  1. Bring 150ml/5fl oz water and the sugar to the boil in a saucepan until the sugar has dissolved. Remove from the heat and leave the syrup to cool.

  2. Pour the syrup into a large bowl. Add the rum and the clementines to the syrup. Cover and store in the fridge for a week before using.

  3. For the upside-down cake, preheat the oven to 180C/160C Fan/Gas 4. Line a 23cm/9in cake tin with baking parchment.

  4. In a large bowl beat together the brown sugar and 100g/3½oz of the butter until smooth. Spread in the base of the lined tin. Arrange the clementine halves on top. Beat the remaining butter together with the rest of the cake ingredients. Pour the batter over the clementines and bake for 35–40 minutes. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool for 10-15 minutes.

  5. Put the marinated clementines into bowls serve with ice cream and a slice of upside-down cake.
